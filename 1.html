Question

Is there any organic way to push competitors ad results to low in Google? What are all the possible ways ?

Answer

Short answer

Impact: immediate clarity

Yes — to some extent. You can't directly force paid Google ads lower using only “organic” tactics because ad placement is decided by the auction (bids + Quality Score). But you can use entirely legitimate, organic and earned strategies to shrink the real estate available to ads, steal user attention, and dominate the page so competitors’ ads become less visible or less effective.

Legitimate, ethical ways

Practical approaches (with impact/time & tips)

9 strategies

Click each strategy to expand details. These are ordered roughly by impact & time horizon.

1) Own more SERP slots
High impact, medium-term → long-term

What: Publish/claim multiple types of content for the same queries so your properties occupy many positions: main site pages, blog posts, PDFs, YouTube videos, images, press/reviews, product pages, social profiles, and a Google Business Profile.

Why it helps: Google shows limited real estate. The more slots you own, the less visual attention and space remain for competitor ads.

How (quick tips): create a comparison page, a long-form pillar article, a short FAQ for featured-snippet chance, and a short video for YouTube. Use descriptive titles and schema (FAQ, Product, Video).

2) Target SERP features that push ads down
Medium → long

What: Aim for Featured Snippets, People Also Ask (PAA), Knowledge Panels, Local Pack, Shopping results or Video carousels.

How:

  • Structure content in Q→A form (short answer first) for snippets.
  • Use FAQ schema for PAA/snippet chances.
  • Optimize Google Business Profile for Local Pack (NAP consistency, photos, reviews).
  • Post product feed (Merchant Center) to appear in Shopping. (Effort: Medium)
3) Strong brand/PR + earned media
Medium → high

What: Get authoritative press mentions, industry pages, and citations covering your brand or product.

Why: News boxes, high-authority links and brand signals increase chance of top SERP placement and occupy news/results sections.

How: press releases to reputable outlets, guest articles, analyst mentions.

4) Content & SEO that matches user intent
Steady, long-term

What: Deep content that satisfies search intent for target keywords (how-to, compare, buy, local, reviews).

How: content clusters, internal linking, E-E-A-T signals (author bios, citations), structured data. Effort: Ongoing

5) Local SEO / Google Business Profile
Fast wins for local queries

What: Optimize GMB fully, collect genuine reviews, use posts, keep hours & photos updated.

Why: Local Pack often appears above organic and sometimes above ads.

Effort: Low→Medium; quick wins with reviews & optimization.

6) Video + image SEO
Quick → medium

What: Publish short video demos and image-optimized assets (YouTube videos often rank high).

How: Short 60–120s videos answering common questions, strong thumbnails, timestamps.

7) Product listings & Shopping presence
Paid + organic blend

What: Populate Google Merchant Center and optimize product pages with schema.

Note: Requires adherence to listing policies but yields prime SERP placement.

8) Brand dominance & defensive content
Medium

What: Dominate brand-related queries with subdomains, microsites, help docs, and social accounts.

Why: Google shows fewer competitor ads on clear brand-owned queries.

9) Complement with targeted paid moves
Honest / fastest

What: Improve your Quality Score (better landing pages, higher CTR, relevant keywords) and adjust bids/targeting.

Why: Paid improvements are the most direct way to reduce competitor ad position quickly.

What not to do

Unethical & illegal tactics (avoid)

Risks: legal, reputation
  • Automated or manual click fraud (clicking competitor ads).
  • Fake DMCA/trademark takedown abuse or false reports.
  • Creating spammy backlinks or negative-SEO campaigns.
  • Posting fake reviews or impersonation.
  • Hacking, social-engineering, or fraud.

These tactics risk legal exposure, account suspension and long-term reputation damage.

Action Plan

Practical 7-step action plan (prioritized)

Start to finish
  1. Audit SERP for 5–10 target queries: note features (ads, local pack, snippets).
  2. Claim/optimize Google Business Profile with photos, services & reviews.
  3. Create featured-snippet–friendly pages (short answer + expanded content + schema).
  4. Publish comparison/review pages addressing buyers’ questions vs competitors.
  5. Launch short YouTube videos answering the same queries and embed them.
  6. Distribute PR / earn authoritative links for pillar content.
  7. Monitor results and optionally supplement with targeted Google Ads bids.

KPIs: SERP feature occupancy, organic impressions & clicks, CTR, conversion rate, and competitor impression/overlap (if running ads).

Final notes

Honest summary

We cannot reliably “push competitors’ ads down” purely by trickery; winning the SERP ethically means owning more of it. Combine short-term tactics (GMB, video, FAQ schema) with long-term investments (authority content, PR, product feeds).

Paid + organic together is the fastest, most reliable approach to reduce competitor ad visibility while building sustainable traffic.
